Taj Mahal

Location: Agra, India

Established: 1632 CE

Architect: Ustad Ahmad Lahori

History

The Taj Mahal was commissioned in 1632 by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his favorite wife, Mumtaz Mahal. The construction was completed in 1648, but some parts of the complex were finished in subsequent years. It is an exquisite example of Mughal architecture, blending elements from Islamic, Persian, Ottoman Turkish, and Indian styles.

Shah Jahan was devastated by the death of Mumtaz Mahal during childbirth, and he vowed to build the most beautiful mausoleum in her honor. The Taj Mahal is built from white marble that reflects hues according to the intensity of sunlight, making it a remarkable sight throughout the day. The complex is set in lush gardens that symbolize paradise, with water channels that enhance its beauty.

In addition to its architectural beauty, the Taj Mahal is rich in symbolism. The central dome, which rises to 35 meters, is surrounded by four smaller domes and minarets, representing the balance of nature. The intricate inlay work of precious stones into the marble, known as pietra dura, showcases the artistry of Mughal craftsmen.

Over the years, the Taj Mahal has faced challenges, including environmental issues and pollution, which have caused deterioration of its marble. Conservation efforts have been made to preserve this iconic structure, ensuring that future generations can appreciate its grandeur. In 1983, the Taj Mahal was designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site, recognizing its cultural significance and universal value.
